After outlining the stability problem for Friedmann–Lemaître–Robertson–Walker (FLRW) spacetimes, we study the wave equation—as a proxy for the Einstein equations—on decelerated FLRW spacetimes with non-compact, flat spatial sections. We demonstrate how dispersion and expansion affect the long-time behaviour of waves. In particular, we present uniform energy bounds and integrated local energy decay estimates across the full decelerated expansion range. Furthermore, we describe a hierarchy of r-weighted energy estimates, in the spirit of the Dafermos–Rodnianski method, which lead to energy decay estimates.
